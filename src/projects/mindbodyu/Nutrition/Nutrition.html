Fruits

What It Is: Foods that are typically sweet or tart in flavor, fresh, canned, frozen, or dried, and may be juiced.

Daily Recommendation: About 2 cups per day. includes fresh, frozen, and canned fruits, but watch for added sugars in some processed fruits.

Examples:

  • Apples
  • Bananas
  • Oranges
  • Grapes
  • Peaches
Protein

What It Is: Foods that are rich in protein, essential for building and repairing body tissue, and providing other nutrients like iron and zinc.

Daily Recommendation: About 5.5 ounces per day.

Examples:

  • Lean meats
  • Poultry
  • Seafood
  • Eggs
  • Nuts
  • Seeds
  • Beans
  • Peas
  • Soy products like tofu and tempeh
Vegetables

What It Is: A wide variety of plant-based foods that come in multiple forms including fresh, canned, frozen, or dried/dehydrated, and may be aten raw or cooked.

Daily Recommendation: About 2.5 cups per day.

Examples:

  • Spinach
  • Carrots
  • Broccoli
  • Sweet potatoes
  • Green beans
Grains

What It Is: Foods made from wheat, rice, oats, cornmeal, barley, or another cereal grain. Grains are divided into two subgroups, whole grains and refined grains.

Daily Recommendation: About 6 ounces per day, with at least half being whole grains.

Examples:

  • Whole wheat bread
  • Brown rice
  • Oatmeal
  • Popcorn
  • Pasta
Dairy

What It Is: Products made from milk that retain their calcium content, including dairy alternatives that are fortified with calcium.

Daily Recommendation: About 3 cups per day.

Examples:

  • Milk
  • Cheese
  • Yogurt
  • Fortified soy milk
Oils & Fats

What It Is: Fats that are liquid at room temperature, not technically a food group but important for providing essential nutrients.

Daily Recommendation: About 5 teaspoons per day.

Examples:

  • Olive oil
  • Canola oil
  • Sunflower oil
  • Foods like avocados, nuts, and seeds that contain these healthy oils
** Individual needs may vary based on factors like age, gender, level of physical activity, and health goals. For more detail information, please visit U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA)

How to Use the Meal Plan Generator?

Welcome to our Meal Plan Generator! Follow these simple steps to create a customized meal plan tailored to your dietary needs and preferences.

  • 1 . Set Your Target Calories: Find the "Target Calories" field. This is where you'll input the total number of calories you aim to consume in a day. (A general guideline is 2000 calories per day for women and 2500 for men.)
  • 2. Choose Your Diet: Locate the "Diet" input box. Here, you can specify any dietary restrictions or preferences you have. Examples include "Vegetarian," "Vegan," "Gluten-Free," "Keto," etc. If you have no specific diet, you can leave this blank.
  • 3. Exclude Ingredients: In the "Excluded Ingredients" field, list any foods you want to avoid in your meal plan. Separate each ingredient with a comma. For example, if you're allergic to nuts and don't like broccoli, you'd enter "nuts, broccoli".
  • 4. Generate Your Plan: Click the "Generate Plan" button. Once you've filled in the necessary information, click the button to create your meal plan.
  • 5. Explore Your Meal Plan: Review the generated meal plan. You'll see a variety of recipes, including their calorie content. Click on any meal to get more details, such as the recipe, ingredients list, and preparation instructions.
